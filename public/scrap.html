Shocking video shows Arsenal fans tearing down stadium banners at White Hart Lane after Tottenham clash

  • Rachel Blundy
  • Thursday 24 September 2015 12:59 BST

Shocking video footage shows Arsenal fans tearing down stadium placards at White Hart Lane and throwing them into the crowd below after their team's Capital One Cup victory over Tottenham last night.

The banners, which appeared to read 'This is my club - the one and only Tottenham Hotspur', were torn down amid cheers from fellow fans and dropped down to people below.

Supporters standing on the stadium's lower tier were then seen punching the discarded placards as stadium security staff struggled to contain the chaos.

Arsenal's 2-1 victory, courtesy of a Mathieu Flamini double, was marred by violence, when the away Gunners' support was held in the stadium at full-time, a standard measure to ensure their own safety.

Sharing a short video of the vandalism on Twitter, one football fan wrote: "Spurs ground is actually getting smashed to pieces". 

Another person said: "I thought they were the most well behaved fans in the world??? #AFC fans ripping banners off at White Hart Lane."

If found guilty, Arsenal fans face lengthy bans over the vandalism on Tottenham's stadium.

Prior to the ugly scenes, the Gunners beat their north London rivals 2-1 in the fourth round Capital One Cup tie.

Ten people have been arrested for over suspected criminal damage, public order and assault offences following last night's match.

A spokesman for the Metropolitan Police would not say whether the arrests related to the attacks on the stadium's banners.

An Arsenal spokesman told Standard Sport: “We’re concerned that this has happened. We will work with Tottenham and the authorities on this.”

Approximately 3,000 Arsenal fans were in attendance at last night’s cup clash and those found guilty of criminal damage could be prosecuted with the additional possibility of a lengthy ban from future Gunners matches.

The Football Association have no specific jurisdiction over supporters, so Arsenal are extremely unlikely to face a fine. Instead, both Arsenal and Spurs will work closely with police to identify those involved, using the stadium’s CCTV cameras and their information on previous offenders.
